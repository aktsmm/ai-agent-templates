## Content Strategy & Planning

### Content Pillars and Themes

Every successful content program is built on 3-5 **content pillars** â€” broad themes that align with your brand expertise and audience interests.

**How to define content pillars:**

1. List your core products/services and unique expertise areas
2. Research what your audience searches for and cares about
3. Identify the intersection of your expertise and audience needs
4. Validate with keyword volume and competitor analysis

**Example pillars for a SaaS company:**

- AI & Automation (thought leadership)
- Customer Success Stories (social proof)
- Industry Trends & Analysis (authority)
- How-To Guides & Tutorials (utility)
- Company Culture & Behind-the-Scenes (brand humanization)

### Editorial Calendar Best Practices

An editorial calendar ensures consistent publishing and strategic content distribution.

**Weekly cadence (recommended starting point):**
| Day | Content Type | Channel |
|-----------|---------------------------|--------------------|
| Monday | Blog post | Blog, LinkedIn |
| Tuesday | Social media carousel | LinkedIn, Instagram|
| Wednesday | Email newsletter | Email |
| Thursday | Short-form social content | Twitter/X, Instagram|
| Friday | Video or infographic | YouTube, LinkedIn |

**Planning best practices:**

- Plan content 4-6 weeks in advance
- Leave 20% capacity for reactive/trending content
- Batch similar content types for production efficiency
- Review and adjust monthly based on performance data
- Align with product launches, events, and seasonal trends

### Audience Persona Development

Build detailed personas to guide content creation:

**Template:**

- **Name**: Marketing Manager Maria
- **Demographics**: 30-45, B2B SaaS companies, 50-500 employees
- **Goals**: Increase lead generation, prove marketing ROI
- **Pain Points**: Limited budget, content creation bottleneck, attribution challenges
- **Content Preferences**: How-to guides, case studies, data-driven reports
- **Channels**: LinkedIn (daily), email newsletters, industry blogs
- **Search Behavior**: "how to [solve problem]", "best [tool/practice] for [use case]"

### Content Audit Process

Regular content audits improve SEO and user experience:

1. **Inventory**: Export all URLs with page titles and metadata
2. **Classify**: Categorize by content type, topic, funnel stage
3. **Analyze**: Check traffic, rankings, engagement, conversion for each
4. **Decide**: Keep, update, merge, or retire each piece
5. **Execute**: Prioritize updates by potential impact
6. **Monitor**: Track performance changes post-update

**Audit frequency**: Quarterly for high-volume blogs, bi-annually for smaller sites.

## Blog Writing Best Practices

### Blog Post Structure

A well-structured blog post improves readability and SEO:

1. **Headline (H1)**: Include primary keyword, 60 characters max, use power words
   - Good: "10 Proven Content Marketing Strategies That Drive Revenue"
   - Weak: "Content Marketing Tips"

2. **Introduction (100-150 words)**:
   - Hook with a statistic, question, or bold statement
   - State the problem the reader faces
   - Preview the solution (thesis statement)

3. **Body sections (H2/H3 headers)**:
   - One main idea per section
   - Use bullet points and numbered lists
   - Include data, examples, and expert quotes
   - Add images/diagrams every 300-400 words

4. **Conclusion (100-150 words)**:
   - Summarize key takeaways
   - Include a clear CTA (call to action)
   - Link to related content

### Blog Writing Guidelines

**Word count recommendations by content type:**
| Content Type | Ideal Length | Purpose |
|-----------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| News/Update | 600-800 words | Timeliness |
| How-To Guide | 1,500-2,500 words | Utility, SEO |
| Listicle | 1,200-2,000 words | Shareability |
| Thought Leadership | 1,000-1,800 words | Authority |
| Case Study | 1,200-2,000 words | Social proof |
| Ultimate Guide | 3,000-5,000 words | SEO, authority |
| Product Comparison | 2,000-3,000 words | Commercial intent |

**Writing best practices:**

- Write at an 8th-grade reading level for general audiences
- Use active voice (aim for 90%+ active sentences)
- Keep paragraphs to 3-4 sentences maximum
- Use transition words between sections
- Include internal links (3-5 per post) and external links (2-3 per post)
- Add alt text to all images
- End with a strong CTA tied to business goals

### Content Formatting

- **Bold** key phrases and important terms
- Use `code formatting` for technical terms when appropriate
- Add table of contents for posts over 2,000 words
- Include a featured image (1200x630px recommended)
- Add social sharing buttons
- Include author bio with photo

## Social Media Content

### Platform-Specific Best Practices

#### LinkedIn

- **Post length**: 1,300 characters max for optimal engagement
- **Best content types**: Carousels (6.1% avg engagement), text posts with line breaks, polls
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant hashtags
- **Best posting times**: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM and 12-1 PM
- **Frequency**: 3-5 posts per week
- **Tips**: Start with a hook line, use line breaks for readability, ask questions to drive comments

#### Twitter/X

- **Post length**: 280 characters (threads for longer content)
- **Best content types**: Threads (3.5% engagement), quote tweets, polls
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 per tweet
- **Best posting times**: Weekdays 12-1 PM, Mon/Thu show highest engagement
- **Frequency**: 3-7 tweets per week (plus replies/retweets)
- **Tips**: Lead with value, use threads for thought leadership, engage in replies

#### Instagram

- **Caption length**: 2,200 character max (first 125 visible without "more")
- **Best content types**: Reels (5.2% engagement), carousels, Stories
- **Hashtags**: 5-15 relevant hashtags (mix of broad and niche)
- **Best posting times**: Mon/Wed/Fri, 11 AM - 1 PM
- **Frequency**: 3-5 feed posts per week, daily Stories
- **Tips**: First line is the hook, use Reels for reach, Stories for engagement

#### Facebook

- **Post length**: 40-80 characters for highest engagement
- **Best content types**: Video (native upload), link posts, live video
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum
- **Best posting times**: Wednesday 11 AM, Friday 10-11 AM
- **Frequency**: 3-5 posts per week
- **Tips**: Native video outperforms YouTube links, ask questions, use Facebook Groups

### Social Media Content Calendar

**Monthly planning template:**

- Week 1: Educational content (how-to, tips, guides)
- Week 2: Social proof (customer stories, testimonials, case studies)
- Week 3: Engagement (polls, questions, behind-the-scenes)
- Week 4: Promotional (product features, offers, events)

### Hashtag Strategy

**Hashtag tiers for maximum reach:**

1. **Branded** (1-2): #YourBrand, #YourCampaign
2. **Industry** (2-3): #ContentMarketing, #DigitalMarketing
3. **Niche** (3-5): #B2BContent, #ContentStrategy, #SEOTips
4. **Trending** (1-2): Relevant trending topics when applicable

## SEO & Search Optimization

### Keyword Research Process

1. **Seed keywords**: Start with 5-10 core terms related to your business
2. **Expand**: Use tools to find related keywords, questions, and long-tail variations
3. **Analyze**: Check search volume, keyword difficulty, and current SERP
4. **Map**: Assign keywords to content pieces and funnel stages
5. **Prioritize**: Focus on high-volume, low-competition opportunities first

**Keyword intent classification:**
| Intent | Example | Content Type |
|-----------------|----------------------------------|-----------------------|
| Informational | "what is content marketing" | Blog post, guide |
| Navigational | "HubSpot blog login" | Landing page |
| Commercial | "best content marketing tools" | Comparison, listicle |
| Transactional | "buy content marketing course" | Product/pricing page |

### On-Page SEO Checklist

For every piece of content, optimize:

- [ ] **Title tag**: Primary keyword, under 60 characters, compelling
- [ ] **Meta description**: Include keyword, under 155 characters, with CTA
- [ ] **URL slug**: Short, descriptive, includes keyword (e.g., `/content-marketing-guide`)
- [ ] **H1 heading**: One per page, includes primary keyword
- [ ] **H2/H3 headings**: Include secondary keywords naturally
- [ ] **First 100 words**: Include primary keyword
- [ ] **Image alt text**: Descriptive, includes keyword where natural
- [ ] **Internal links**: 3-5 links to related content
- [ ] **External links**: 2-3 links to authoritative sources
- [ ] **Schema markup**: Article, FAQ, or HowTo schema as appropriate
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly**: Responsive design, readable on all devices
- [ ] **Page speed**: Load time under 3 seconds (Core Web Vitals)

### Content Gap Analysis

Find topics your competitors rank for that you don't:

1. Identify top 5 competitors in your content space
2. Export their top-ranking pages and keywords
3. Compare against your keyword portfolio
4. Identify gaps where competitor ranks but you don't
5. Prioritize gaps by search volume and business relevance
6. Create content plan to fill highest-priority gaps

### Link Building Strategies

- **Guest posting**: Write for industry publications with do-follow links
- **Resource pages**: Get listed on curated resource lists
- **Original research**: Publish surveys, studies, and data reports
- **Expert roundups**: Feature industry experts who will share the content
- **Broken link building**: Find broken links on relevant sites and offer your content
- **HARO/Qwoted**: Respond to journalist queries for media mentions

## Analytics & Performance

### Content Marketing KPIs

Track these metrics to measure content effectiveness:

**Awareness metrics:**

- Organic traffic (sessions, users)
- Social media reach and impressions
- Brand mention volume
- Email subscriber growth

**Engagement metrics:**

- Time on page / Average session duration
- Pages per session
- Social engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Email open rate and click-through rate
- Bounce rate

**Conversion metrics:**

- Lead generation (form fills, downloads)
- Email sign-ups from content
- Content-attributed pipeline
- Content-attributed revenue

**SEO metrics:**

- Keyword rankings (position changes)
- Domain authority / Domain rating
- Backlinks acquired
- Organic click-through rate

### Reporting Cadence

| Report Type       | Frequency | Key Metrics                          |
| ----------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Content Scorecard | Weekly    | Posts published, traffic, engagement |
| Channel Report    | Bi-weekly | Per-channel performance vs goals     |
| SEO Dashboard     | Monthly   | Rankings, organic traffic, backlinks |
| Content ROI       | Quarterly | Pipeline, revenue attribution, CAC   |
| Strategy Review   | Quarterly | Pillar performance, audience growth  |

### Content Performance Benchmarks

**Blog benchmarks (B2B):**

- Average time on page: 3-5 minutes
- Bounce rate: 40-60%
- Organic traffic share: 50-70% of total
- Conversion rate: 2-5% (email sign-up), 1-3% (lead form)

**Social media benchmarks:**

- LinkedIn engagement rate: 2-4% (good), 4%+ (excellent)
- Twitter engagement rate: 1-2% (good), 2%+ (excellent)
- Instagram engagement rate: 3-5% (good), 5%+ (excellent)
