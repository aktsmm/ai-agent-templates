# HR Onboarding Guide

## Overview

This guide covers the complete onboarding process for new hires, from pre-boarding
through the first 90 days. Follow the checklists and procedures below to ensure a
smooth and welcoming experience for every new team member.

---

### Document Collection & Compliance

**Pre-Start Date Documents (Due: 5 business days before start)**

1. **Employment Contract**
   - Review and sign the offer letter and employment agreement
   - Return signed copies via DocuSign or in person to HR
   - Keep a personal copy for your records

2. **Tax Forms**
   - W-4 (Federal Tax Withholding): Complete via the HR portal
   - State tax withholding form (varies by location)
   - For international employees: W-8BEN or applicable treaty form
   - Deadline: Must be submitted before first payroll run

3. **ID Verification (I-9)**
   - Bring original documents (passport, driver's license, etc.)
   - List A: Passport OR List B + C: Driver's license + Social Security card
   - Must be completed within 3 business days of start date
   - Remote employees: Schedule a notary verification or video I-9 service

4. **Bank Details for Payroll**
   - Submit direct deposit form with routing and account numbers
   - Can set up via the HR portal under "Payroll Settings"
   - Changes take effect in the next payroll cycle

5. **Emergency Contact Information**
   - Provide at least one emergency contact
   - Include name, relationship, phone number, and email
   - Submit via the HR portal under "Personal Information"

6. **Benefits Enrollment**
   - Health insurance, dental, vision selection
   - 401(k) / retirement plan enrollment
   - Life insurance and disability coverage
   - Enrollment window: 30 days from start date
   - Benefits information session: Scheduled during Week 1 orientation

**IT Account Setup**

1. **Email & Microsoft 365**
   - Account created 2 days before start date
   - Credentials sent to personal email
   - First login: Change password and set up MFA
   - Install Microsoft Teams, Outlook, OneDrive

2. **VPN Access**
   - GlobalProtect VPN client pre-installed on laptop
   - Configuration guide provided on first day
   - Required for remote work and accessing internal resources

3. **Badge & Physical Access**
   - Photo taken during orientation (Day 1)
   - Badge activated for assigned office areas
   - Remote employees: Badge mailed to home address (if applicable)

4. **Software & Licenses**
   - Standard software suite installed on laptop
   - Role-specific software: Request via IT Service Desk
   - License activation: Follow the setup guide in the IT Wiki

5. **System Permissions**
   - File shares and SharePoint sites: Based on department
   - Application access: Manager approves via access request form
   - Admin/elevated access: Requires additional security clearance

### Training & Development Programs

**Week 1: Orientation**

- **Day 1: Welcome & Company Overview**
  - Company history, mission, and values (2 hours)
  - Office tour and facility introduction (1 hour)
  - HR policies overview: Code of conduct, PTO, expenses (1 hour)
  - IT setup and systems walkthrough (2 hours)
  - Welcome lunch with team

- **Day 2: Compliance Training**
  - Security awareness training (1 hour, mandatory)
  - Harassment prevention training (1 hour, mandatory)
  - Data privacy and GDPR/CCPA basics (1 hour, mandatory)
  - Workplace safety and emergency procedures (30 min)

- **Day 3-5: Department Introduction**
  - Meet the team and key stakeholders
  - Review team goals and current projects
  - Shadow a team member
  - Set up development environment (for engineering roles)

**Week 2-4: Ramp-Up**

- Role-specific technical training
- Product/service deep-dive sessions
- Customer/user persona training
- Internal tools and workflow training
- First 1:1 with manager (set 30/60/90-day goals)

**Month 2-3: Integration**

- Advanced role-specific training
- Cross-team collaboration projects
- Mentorship program activities
- 60-day check-in with HR
- 90-day performance review

### Buddy Program & Social Integration

**Buddy Program Overview**

Every new hire is assigned an onboarding buddy — an experienced employee who
serves as an informal guide during the first 90 days. The buddy is NOT the
new hire's manager; they are a peer who helps with day-to-day questions.

**Matching Criteria**

- Same or similar department (preferred)
- Similar role level (peer, not senior management)
- Same office location (for in-person buddies)
- Shared interests or background (when possible)
- Buddies must have 6+ months tenure at the company

**Buddy Responsibilities**

1. **Pre-Start**: Send a welcome message via email
2. **Day 1**: Meet for coffee/tea and office tour
3. **Week 1**: Daily 15-minute check-ins
4. **Week 2-4**: Weekly 30-minute catch-ups
5. **Month 2-3**: Bi-weekly informal chats
6. **Day 90**: Graduation lunch and program feedback
